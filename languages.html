Languages of India

The States Reorganisation Act of 1956 organised state borders in India along the linguistic lines. The act gave precedence to languages with printed literature. It did not consider other languages despite their rich oral tradition. Apart from Hindi, with its wide usage in northern states, and English - a major second languages of India, the state languages are the most spoken languages in India.

In 1950, the eighth schedule to the Constitution listed 14 official languages for the country. By 2011, the list increased to 22 languages, and now there are demands for inclusion of 39 more languages. Still, The Indian language spectrum is much larger than this list. As per ethnologue.com, there are 464 languages in India. The people’s linguistic survey takes the count to 781. The differences between various studies is due to the ambiguity around the appellations ‘language’ and ‘dialect’.
